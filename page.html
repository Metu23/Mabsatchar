The origins of achaar can be traced back to ancient India, where it was a popular condiment made by pickling fruits and vegetables in a mixture of spices and salt. The use of pickling was an effective way to preserve food, especially during the monsoon season when fresh produce was scarce.

Atchar

A spicy condiment similar to a chutney in consistency and usually made with lime or mango. Atchar goes hand in hand with Indian or South African dishes and is especially delicious on a kota, african cuisines and many more dishes.

The complex savoury flavours of the atchar help to enhance the umami of the main dishes with which it is served. To give the dishes a little more body, several complementary toppings are normally added to it, such as green onion, garlic, and hot peppers.
